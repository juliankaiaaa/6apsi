# Final Project Planning - How it is graded

This planning submission is worth **100 points**, split evenly across the four
documents you produce. It is graded by your instructor from the documents
themselves (paper photos, Docs, or files are all fine) - there is no autograder.
Planning is judged on being **specific, honest, and internally consistent**, not
on being "right": a modest project planned clearly beats an ambitious one planned
vaguely.

| Part | Document | Points |
| - | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Proposal](01-proposal.md) | 25 |
| 2 | [Wireframes & components](02-wireframes.md) | 25 |
| 3 | [Design System](03-design-system.md) | 25 |
| 4 | [Reflection Journal](04-reflection-journal.md) | 25 |
| | **Total** | **100** |

Each part is scored on a sliding scale; the levels below are the guide. A part
left blank or clearly rushed scores in the "Needs work" band regardless of the
rest.

---

## Part 1 - Proposal (25 points)

| Criterion | Max | Excellent | Satisfactory | Needs work |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Purpose & audience** | 8 | one specific purpose sentence and a named, concrete audience | a real but broad purpose or audience | "an app about X" / "for everyone" |
| **Sections or routes** | 7 | 3 to 5 screens, each with a clear reason to exist | listed but some overlap or padding | missing, or a wall of unfocused screens |
| **State & content plan** | 7 | names the data each screen owns and the real content to gather | partial: some state or content identified | no thought given to data or content |
| **One risk** | 3 | a genuine, specific uncertainty named | a vague risk | none |

## Part 2 - Wireframes & components (25 points)

| Criterion | Max | Excellent | Satisfactory | Needs work |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Screen map / flow** | 7 | every screen and navigation drawn; a clear entry point and no dead ends | mostly mapped, a gap or two | no map, or broken navigation |
| **Box sketches (2 widths)** | 7 | each screen sketched at phone and desktop, with what stacks marked | sketched at one width only | thin or missing sketches |
| **Component breakdown** | 8 | boxes sorted into a sensible component tree (atoms / molecules / organisms); repeats named once | some components named, tree partial | boxes not turned into components |
| **Labeling & legibility** | 3 | every block labeled; someone else could build from it | mostly labeled | unlabeled, hard to read |

## Part 3 - Design System (25 points)

| Criterion | Max | Excellent | Satisfactory | Needs work |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Styling approach & tokens** | 8 | a stack chosen; color, type, and spacing set as named tokens | tokens defined but incomplete or stack unstated | ad hoc values, no tokens |
| **Color & contrast** | 6 | 3 to 5 colors with roles; contrast checked at 4.5:1 | palette set, contrast not verified | too many colors or unreadable pairs |
| **Reusable components** | 7 | components pulled from the wireframes, each with a level and props | some components listed | none, or invented in the abstract |
| **Responsive & accessibility plan** | 4 | breakpoints noted; the accessibility checklist worked through | partly addressed | ignored |

## Part 4 - Reflection Journal (25 points)

| Criterion | Max | Excellent | Satisfactory | Needs work |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Coverage of the course** | 7 | speaks to JS, React, and styling in the student's own words | covers some modules | generic or skips most of the course |
| **Honesty & specificity** | 8 | concrete examples of what clicked and what is still shaky | some specifics, some filler | vague, could describe anyone |
| **Connection to the project** | 7 | ties the learning directly to the plan in Docs 1 to 3 | a loose connection | no link to the project |
| **Effort & length** | 3 | thoughtful, in the 400 to 600 word range | present but thin | a few lines |

---

## The band, in short

- **Excellent (23 to 25 per part):** specific, honest, consistent across the four
  documents; the plan could be handed to someone else to build.
- **Satisfactory (15 to 22):** the work is there but broad, partial, or uneven.
- **Needs work (0 to 14):** blank, rushed, or so vague it does not guide the
  build.

> The point of this submission is to make the build easier, not to produce a
> polished document for its own sake. Plan honestly, and the project itself gets
> faster and better.
